At **Neuroinformatics 2018** in Montréal, I made the case for better data
management in precision-medicine research — using Alzheimer's anti-amyloid trials
as a motivating example of how much rides on trustworthy, integrable data.

The core of the talk was **GREX**, a system to record, track, store, find, and
retrieve computational results (including data) in a FAIR — Findable, Accessible,
Interoperable, Reusable — manner, so researchers can discover, trust, and share
exploratory analyses across collaborative studies.
